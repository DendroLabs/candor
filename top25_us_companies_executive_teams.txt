COMMON C-SUITE ROLES FOUND ACROSS MOST COMPANIES:
- CEO (25/25) -- Universal
- CFO (25/25) -- Universal
- General Counsel / CLO (24/25) -- Nearly universal (Berkshire created this role only in 2026)
- CIO/CTO (20/25) -- Very common, sometimes combined
- CHRO / Chief People Officer (20/25) -- Common, though titles vary widely
- CMO / Chief Brand Officer (15/25) -- Common but not universal

EMERGING/NON-STANDARD EXECUTIVE ROLES (2025-2026 trends):
1. Chief AI Officer / VP of AI (Apple, Amazon, Microsoft)
2. Chief Digital Officer (Coca-Cola -- newly created 2026)
3. Chief Data & Analytics Officer (JPMorgan Chase)
4. Chief Growth Officer (Walmart)
5. Chief Impact Officer (Salesforce)
6. Chief Sustainability Officer (P&G, Coca-Cola incorporated into broader roles)
7. Chief Innovation Officer (UnitedHealth Group)
8. Chief Content Officer (Netflix)
9. Chief Talent Officer vs. Chief People Officer vs. CHRO (varying titles for same function)
10. Chief Product Officer (Meta, Salesforce, Mastercard, Netflix)
11. Chief Revenue Officer (Meta, Salesforce)
12. Chief Commercial Officer (AbbVie)
13. Chief Ethics and Compliance Officer (Eli Lilly, UnitedHealth)
14. Internal "CEO" designations for business units (Microsoft, Amazon, JPMorgan, Walmart, P&G)

COMPANIES WITH NOTABLY LEAN C-SUITES:
- Berkshire Hathaway (minimalist structure)
- Tesla (very few named officers)
- NVIDIA (historically lean, now adding roles like CMO)
- ExxonMobil (operational president structure, few "Chief" titles)

COMPANIES WITH MOST EXTENSIVE C-SUITES:
- Procter & Gamble (Chief Brand Officer, Chief Design Officer, Chief Sustainability Officer, Chief Analytics/Insights/Media Officer, etc.)
- Coca-Cola (numerous "Global Chief" roles)
- Salesforce (multiple President + Chief Officer dual titles)
- JPMorgan Chase (large Operating Committee)
