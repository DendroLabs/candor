# Standardized Executive Roles Analysis

## Source: Top 25 US Companies by Market Cap (2026)

Companies analyzed: Apple, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Amazon, Alphabet/Google, Meta, Berkshire Hathaway, Tesla, Broadcom, Eli Lilly, JPMorgan Chase, Walmart, Visa, UnitedHealth Group, Mastercard, Johnson & Johnson, Costco, Procter & Gamble, ExxonMobil, Home Depot, AbbVie, Netflix, Salesforce, Coca-Cola, Bank of America

---

## ROLE MAPPING: Raw Titles → Standardized Categories

### 1. CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER (CEO)
**Prevalence:** 25/25 (Universal)

| Variant Titles Found | Companies |
|---|---|
| CEO | All 25 |
| Co-CEO | Netflix |
| Chairman & CEO | JPMorgan, P&G, J&J, Coca-Cola, BofA, ExxonMobil, Eli Lilly |
| President & CEO | Costco, Mastercard, Walmart |
| Founder/Chairman/CEO | Meta, Salesforce |
| Technoking of Tesla (+ CEO) | Tesla |

**Core Responsibilities Observed:**
- Ultimate strategic direction and vision
- Board relationship and corporate governance
- Capital allocation and M&A decisions
- Public representation and stakeholder management
- Culture setting and organizational design
- Executive team composition and succession planning

### 20. DIVISION / BUSINESS UNIT CEO
**Prevalence:** ~15/25 (Increasingly common)

| Variant Titles Found | Companies |
|---|---|
| CEO, [Business Unit] | Microsoft, Amazon, JPMorgan, Walmart, P&G, Salesforce |
| President, [Business Unit] | ExxonMobil, Coca-Cola, Eli Lilly |
| Worldwide Chairman, [Segment] | J&J, Eli Lilly |

**Core Responsibilities Observed:**
- Full P&L ownership for business unit
- Business unit strategy and execution
- Unit-level talent and culture
- Product/service portfolio for the unit
- Unit-level customer relationships
- Cross-unit collaboration
- Local market adaptation
